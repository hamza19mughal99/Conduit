The Refrigerant Cycle

Units use refrigerant – a fluid that absorbs heat from the environment that can cool when combined with compressors and evaporators. Compressors increase the pressure of a gas, by reducing volume, while evaporators convert the state of the refrigerant from liquid to gas, as it absorbs heat. In heating mode, a refrigeration cycle pumps heat from a low-temperature level to a higher one.

Heat pumps in action

AIRCONDITIONING

For heat pumps in air conditioning mode, electricity is used to change the pressure of the refrigerant to absorb heat.


At low pressures, the refrigerant absorbs heat available in the air and evaporates from a liquid to a gas. At high pressures, a gas refrigerant is higher energy than the outside air, so it passes heat to the surrounding air and the refrigerant condenses back to a liquid when it cools.

HEATING

In the winter, a heat pump reverses – it pulls heat from the outside and transfers it into your home.


Even at cold outdoor temperatures, air contains heat energy. The refrigerant absorbs that heat and evaporates. As in the air conditioning cycle, the refrigerant can be pressurized, which raises the temperature. As the refrigerant is pumped into the home, it warms the home until heat is extracted and it condenses back into a liquid, and the cycle continues.
